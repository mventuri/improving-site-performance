What is WordPress site performance?

WordPress site performance is characterized by how fast pages on the site load. There are several reasons why performance matters:

  1. Better performance brings better visitor/customer experience
  2. Google and other search engines rank sites based on performance, so that they rank sites with better performance higher
  3. Adequate site response site helps to decrease bounce rate, the rate at which users "bounce off" your site and go somewhere else

Some Stats*...

  1. 40% of people abandon a website that takes more than 3 seconds to load
  2. A one second delay in page response can result in a 7% reduction in conversions.
  3. 47% of consumers expect a web page to load in 2 seconds or less.
So it makes sense to invest some time to ensure your site is optimized and performs well.
*source: NeilPatel.com

use one of the sites which provide speed measurement services:

  • Google PageSpeed
  • GTMetrix
  • DareBoost

What can impede performance?

there are two primary bottlenecks that may slow down data that's being transferred between your server and your visitors:

  1. Host: the kind of hosting you choose has a great impact on your site's speed.
  2. Network: sometimes the network your visitor uses to access your site is not ideal

How to optimize your website? (1 of 2)

  1. Caching: it is the process of storing data from previous requests to be re-used for subsequent requests. Caching prevents repeating database requests that transfer the same data over and over.
  2. Offloading: Another way to approach the server load problem and increase the speed of content delivery is to offload part of the content to other servers. One can use a CDN for this. CDN stands for Content Delivery Network. A CDN is a network of servers, usually located at various sites around the world.

How to optimize your website? (2 of 2)

  1. Optimizing your site's configuration:A fast, lightweight theme will perform much better than a heavy graphic-laden theme. And if you have a lot of plugins activated, that may also take a toll on performance.
  2. Compressing: You also can minify your content so that your pages are delivered more quickly to readers' browsers. Minification removes all unnecessary or redundant data from the code without affecting performance, minimizing the size of the file that needs to download to the user's browser.

Summary

Your site or blog's performance is very important for its visitor experience and for the ranking it gets from search engines. The pillars for improving the site speed are:

  1. choosing a reliable and powerful hosting
  2. enabling caching and compression
  3. making sure your site uses lightweight theme and is not overloaded with plugins
  4. possibly using external services to offload part of your bandwidth
